A little bit about me

My name is Cheryl Miyake, and I’m passionate about inspiring and empowering people through innovative learning experiences. I am constantly learning from the people around me and sharing new found knowledge and skills with others.

My experience as a designer, educator, and instructional technology leader enables me to empathize and advocate for youth, teachers, policy makers, and families. Growing up on an island and moving to the mainland for school and work allows me to understand the importance of grassroots efforts and bring them to scale.

I strive to apply UX/UI design principles to everyday problems at work and in my spare time. I’m a big-picture thinker and aim to design experiences end-to-end, from empathizing with users to measuring key performance indicators.

My values

🤓Always stay curious

Humans are built to learn. We learn best when we feel comfortable to take risks, learn with others, and have agency to follow our curiosities. I am constantly striving to stay within my Zone of Proximal Development via travel, partnering with and mentoring others, listening to podcasts, reading up on new design and pedagogical trends, and getting my hands dirty with new projects.

😍Inspire through passion

Which do you prefer: the carrot or the stick? Intrinsic motivation > extrinsic motivation. I model excitement and inquiry to inspire change in others. I’m blessed to be surrounded by empathic and passionate individuals striving to make our world a more inclusive and vibrant place. I am only one person, and I only have one life to live. I strive to live my best life and empower others to do the same.

🤝🏼Innovate through empathy and equity

I do not know everything, but I’m always trying to learn from others. Working in communities and asking crucial questions helps me to empathize with people and advocate for their needs. It’s important to scale grassroots efforts - people impacted by problems should have the key to their solutions. I design for all fringe cases to create a more equitable society where we all can succeed.

🌎Change the world sustainably

Each of us change everyday. We may not always want or be ready for change. Having a growth mindset and big-picture point of view helps me to positively respond to change. I sustainably design solutions by reflecting on past mistakes and garnering insights. Weighing all potential outcomes and their impact on people help me prioritize the best solutions.

Testimonials

Cheryl Miyake is a highly valued member of our diverse Cherry Chase School community who has taught 4th grade here since August 2014. For all of those years I have had the privilege to work with Cheryl as her principal and have greatly appreciated her work with students and their families, her collaboration with her grade-level team of five teachers, and her contributions to our school community through a variety of district-level leadership roles in addition to her general willingness to share her own knowledge and new learning with others.

A passionate, enthusiastic teacher who is deeply committed to student success, Cheryl creates an effective learning environment where students are held to high academic expectations as well as supported with their social-emotional needs. She has been a strong proponent of the growth mindset within her classroom as she helps students accept mistakes and learn from them. Cheryl has created routines in her classroom that are equitably implemented, fostering a sense of purpose and order as all students work to meet or exceed 4th grade Common Core State Standards, often in small groups that are differentiated according to student need.

Cheryl clearly understands that families are our partners in education and welcomes them into her classroom as volunteers and as witnesses to the shared learning of our students at publishing parties and other events. She communicates regularly with families through a classroom website, emails, and formal meetings – all with a mind to supporting and celebrating student learning. As we have moved toward deeper integration of technology into instruction, Cheryl has provided significant leadership in this area: first by embracing the tools that we provided for use in the classroom (e.g., Chromebooks, iPads, Apple TV) and then by collaborating with her teammates to identify and implement appropriate ways of utilizing those with students. She has extended that grade-level work in her role as one of our Instructional Technology Lead Teachers, as she models use of tech tools in staff professional development activities and facilitates a Student Tech Team that provides leadership in activities such as Hour of Code and supports peers with myriad tech applications in the classroom. More specific information on the apps she uses is available on her resume.

Cheryl Miyake has a strong desire to make learning relevant and to provide creative activities for students that go beyond the basic curriculum. She seeks to educate and empower students to be lifelong learners, as she herself is.

- Anita Lee, Former Cherry Chase Elementary Principal
